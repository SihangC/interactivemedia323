WINTER                                                                                                                   Home                                                                                                                   SPRING

  1. The sail is so vast when it's laid out on the driveway.
    •                                                                         I stake it with a screwdriver through the shackleat the tack to stretch it smooth, pulling on the head and clew.
  2. Now it's smooth as a night's worth of new snow.
    •                                                                     My wife, my partner, has been torn from her busy day.
  3. We face each other across the sail's foot and with my right hand and her left hand
    •                                                                                                                        (I'm right handed, she's left handed)
  4. we pull an arm's length of the sail down over itself,
    •                                                                        then do this again,
  5. keeping my left hand,
    •                          and her right hand, towards the foot.
  6. Each fold is easier since the sail grows narrower near the top.
    •                                                                                      Then we fold towards each other and I wrap my arms around it,
  7. while she holds the bag's mouth open,
    •                                                     the gray bag that will cover it through the winter.
  8. Then I thank her.
    •                   And the driveway is visible again as it is in spring,
  9. when all the snow has melted.
